In baseball, it's difficult to compare pitchers and batters in their worth and performances. In addition, typical stats like Earned Run Average (ERA) for pitchers and batting average (AVG) for batters don't always tell the whole picture for each respective player.

THEN CAME WAR:

WAR (Winning Above Replacement), provides a homogeneous metric for evaluating both pitchers and batters' performances at the same time. This statistic, along with with advanced performance variables such as wOBA and FIP, allow for us to gain a more accurate representation of the best players in Major League Baseball. These will be discussed in more detail below as we analyze players from the 2016-2017 MLB season.

What makes a good batter?





wOBA is a solid indicator for overall offensive performance by a batter. It is meant to better the metric of On-base plus slugging (OPS), a popular sabermetric, by combining and taking into account all aspects of hitting and weighting them in proportion to their actual run value. If you look at the top graph showing the wOBA values for each player, you can see that a lot of players who rank top in the wOBA value also happened to be a top MVP candidate. Therefore, we can see that top-ranking batters tend to have high wOBA values too. Try clicking on a bar or searching up a player on the barchart to see where they stand on HR vs. WAR chart!




On the lower right is a scatterplot showing the relationship between Home Runs and WAR. It isn't a strong relationship. You can see this by hovering over the trendline of the scatterplot and observing the R-squared value (0.1); value closer to 1 indicates stronger relationship. So Home Runs don't explain everything after all.

WAR: WAR stand for Wins Above Replacement. As Fangraphs states, it attempts to “summarize a player’s total contributions to their team in one statistic”, expressing the value as the amount of Wins they contributed to their team. We use Fangraphs’ formula for this, which takes sabermetrics representing different aspects in batting and pitching, such as fielding, batting, overall league performance. Exact formula for batters: https://bit.ly/2Q7p1LF Exact formula for pitchers: https://bit.ly/2OTbFho WAR should be used as a guide for separating groups of players, and not as an exact estimate. It's hard to distinguish a 6.4 WAR player with 6.1 WAR player using WAR, but WAR can show with high level of confidence that a 6.4 WAR player and 4.1 WAR player are different in their values to quite an extent!
